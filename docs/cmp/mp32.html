MP32: All-index Powerramp Candidate

Status: advanced candidate in the current jmMSE analysis. MP32 has not been adopted. This page reflects the exact jmMSE version 94d4d325ba46220f56097b3de90824c83b7cf3cd and the 25 July 2026 presentation.

3 Rule

Let \(I_t\) be the annual mean of the standardized index information and

\[ x_t = \frac{I_t-0.1}{1.42-0.1}. \]

Before applying the annual change limit, the current power-ramp rule calculates the catch recommendation as

\[ C_t^* = \begin{cases} 270, & I_t \le 0.1,\\ 270 + (1500-270)x_t^{2.5}, & 0.1 < I_t < 1.42,\\ 1500(0.2+0.8x_t)^{0.6}, & I_t \ge 1.42. \end{cases} \]

Unlike a capped hockeystick, the upper branch continues to rise above the trigger. The annual change rule then prevents the result from falling below 85% or rising above 120% of the previous recommendation. For the first application year, the reference value is 1385.

4 Evidence status

The July presentation identifies MP32 as the optimal powerramp minimum/limit run. The MP29-MP32 comparison reports broadly similar long-term catch and stock performance, with MP32 showing more year-to-year catch variation (IACC) than MP29. The jmMSE robustness analysis carries both candidates forward.

Primary sources: model_tune.R, model_robustness.R, and the July presentation source.

5 Annual application requirements still to resolve

  • authoritative provider and annual cutoff for each index;
  • precise index standardization performed by cpues.ind and the software version used;
  • treatment of missing or revised indices;
  • catch units and rounding;
  • approved catch-allocation update rule; and
  • exceptional-circumstances review and decision process.

## Rule

Let $I_t$ be the annual mean of the standardized index information and

$$
x_t = \frac{I_t-0.1}{1.42-0.1}.
$$

Before applying the annual change limit, the current power-ramp rule calculates
the catch recommendation as

$$
C_t^* =
\begin{cases}
270, & I_t \le 0.1,\\
270 + (1500-270)x_t^{2.5}, & 0.1 < I_t < 1.42,\\
1500(0.2+0.8x_t)^{0.6}, & I_t \ge 1.42.
\end{cases}
$$

Unlike a capped hockeystick, the upper branch continues to rise above the
trigger. The annual change rule then prevents the result from falling below 85%
or rising above 120% of the previous recommendation. For the first application
year, the reference value is 1385.

## Evidence status

The July presentation identifies MP32 as the optimal powerramp minimum/limit
run. The MP29-MP32 comparison reports broadly similar long-term catch and stock
performance, with MP32 showing more year-to-year catch variation (IACC) than
